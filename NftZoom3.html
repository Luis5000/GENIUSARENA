Egg Stage (0-2 weeks)

Fertilized eggs: Translucent white with a visible dark spot (developing embryo).

Water: Clean, cool (around 16°C / 60°F), gentle aeration.

No feeding required: Nutrients come from the yolk sac.

Hatchling Stage (2-6 weeks)

External gills: Develop feathery external gills for respiration.

Minimal movement: Remain mostly immobile on the aquarium floor.

Microworms/Brine shrimp: Introduce live food once they start swimming freely.

Juvenile Stage (6 weeks - 4 months)

Rapid growth: Body length increases significantly.

Limb development: Tiny forelimbs and hindlimbs begin to emerge.

Diet: Introduce bloodworms, chopped earthworms, or formulated axolotl pellets.

Adolescent Stage (4 months - 1 year)

Fully formed limbs: All four limbs are well-developed and functional.

Sexual maturity: Around 12-18 months old.

Diet: Continue feeding bloodworms, earthworms, or pellets, maintaining a varied diet.

Adult Stage (1 year+)

Sexual maturity: Can breed throughout the year.

Lifespan: 10-15 years with proper care.

Diet: Maintain a balanced diet of bloodworms, earthworms, and pellets.
