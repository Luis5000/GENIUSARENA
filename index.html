Mission

To preserve and protect the biodiversity of Mexican axolotls through the implementation of blockchain technology. By doing so, we ensure these iconic species continue to thrive in their natural habitat and contribute to the ecological balance of the Xochimilco canals.



Vision

Through our comprehensive platform, we aim to empower scientists, natural resource managers, and the wider community to make informed decisions and collaborate in the protection of these fascinating amphibians. Together, we will work towards a healthier and more sustainable Xochimilco for generations to come.

About the Axolotl
Ambystoma Mexicanum

It is an animal endemic to an area full of tradition and culture known as Lake Xochimilco, which means that it is not found naturally or wild anywhere else on the planet. Of the class Amphibia, the family Ambystomatidae, and the phylum Chordata, whose name comes from the Nahuatl words "atl" and "xolotl" meaning "strange water monster," it has become one of the most emblematic species of Mexico, recognized worldwide.


This place located in Mexico City is a floating garden known as a "Chinampa", it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site home to more than 200 species of birds, 40 species of reptiles and amphibians, and 800 species of plants.

However, many human activities have caused the axolotl population to decline by 99% in the last 25 years!

From 6,000 individuals per square meter in 1998 to less than 60 individuals today, according to UNAM data.


The main threats to their survival are:

habitat loss since the wet lands have been greatly reduced in size due to urbanization and pollution, followed by the pollution of the canals with sewage and agricultural runoff and the introduction of invasive especies to the environment causing them to outcompete for food and habitat.
